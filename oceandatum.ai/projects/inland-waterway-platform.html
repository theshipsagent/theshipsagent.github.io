Econometric Models

1. Lock Delay Cost Model

Calculates the economic impact of lock delays on transportation costs and commodity pricing.

Primary Cost Function:
C(d) = C₀ + α·d + β·d² + γ·S + δ·W

Where:
  C(d) = Total delay cost ($)
  C₀   = Base transportation cost
  d    = Delay time (hours)
  α    = Linear delay cost coefficient
  β    = Quadratic delay cost coefficient (congestion effects)
  γ    = Seasonal adjustment factor
  S    = Seasonal indicator (0-1)
  δ    = Weather impact coefficient
  W    = Weather severity index
Cost Components:
Total Cost = Fuel Cost + Labor Cost + Opportunity Cost + Demurrage

Fuel Cost       = F·d·P_fuel
Labor Cost      = L·d·W_labor
Opportunity     = V·r·d/24
Demurrage       = D·max(0, d - d_free)

Where:
  F       = Fuel consumption rate (gal/hr)
  P_fuel  = Fuel price ($/gal)
  L       = Labor hours per delay hour
  W_labor = Wage rate ($/hr)
  V       = Cargo value ($)
  r       = Daily interest rate
  D       = Demurrage rate ($/day)
  d_free  = Free time allowed (hours)
Base Rates: $8,500 - $12,000 per tow
Delay Impact: $850 - $1,200 per hour
Quadratic Effect: 15-25% cost increase at high delays
Seasonal Variation: ±18% across harvest/low-water periods

2. Vector Autoregression (VAR) Model

Multi-segment time series model capturing temporal dependencies and spillover effects between river segments.

VAR(p) Specification:
Y_t = c + Φ₁·Y_(t-1) + Φ₂·Y_(t-2) + ... + Φₚ·Y_(t-p) + ε_t

Where:
  Y_t = [y₁_t, y₂_t, ..., y_k_t]' (k × 1 vector of rates at time t)
  c   = (k × 1) vector of constants
  Φᵢ  = (k × k) coefficient matrices for lag i
  ε_t = (k × 1) vector of error terms
  p   = Lag order (optimal: 7 days)
Individual Segment Equation:
y_i,t = c_i + Σ(j=1 to k) Σ(l=1 to p) φ_ij,l · y_j,t-l + ε_i,t

Where:
  y_i,t     = Rate for segment i at time t
  φ_ij,l    = Effect of segment j at lag l on segment i
  ε_i,t     ~ N(0, σᵢ²)

Cross-segment effects:
  φ_ij,l ≠ 0 for i ≠ j captures spillover effects
Forecast Generation:
Ŷ_(t+h) = c + Σ(i=1 to p) Φᵢ · Ŷ_(t+h-i)    for h = 1, 2, ..., H

Forecast Variance:
  Var(Ŷ_(t+h)) = Σ(i=0 to h-1) Ψᵢ · Σ_ε · Ψᵢ'

Where:
  Ψᵢ   = Impulse response matrices
  Σ_ε  = Error covariance matrix
  H    = Forecast horizon (7 days)
Segments: 27 river segments modeled simultaneously
Lag Order: 7 days (optimal by AIC/BIC)
Parameters: ~5,100 estimated coefficients
Forecast Horizon: 7-day rolling forecasts
RMSE: $0.85/ton baseline accuracy
R² (in-sample): 0.78 average across segments

3. Spatial Vector Autoregression (Spatial VAR)

Incorporates spatial dependencies using river distance and connectivity to improve forecast accuracy.

Spatial VAR Specification:
Y_t = c + ρ·W·Y_t + Φ₁·Y_(t-1) + ... + Φₚ·Y_(t-p) + θ·W·Y_(t-1) + ε_t

Where:
  W     = (k × k) spatial weight matrix
  ρ     = Spatial autoregressive parameter
  θ     = Spatial lag coefficient
  W·Y_t = Contemporaneous spatial effects
  W·Y_(t-1) = Lagged spatial effects
Spatial Weight Matrix Construction:
W_ij = {
  exp(-d_ij / λ)  if segment i and j are connected
  0               otherwise
}

Row-standardized: w_ij = W_ij / Σ_j W_ij

Where:
  d_ij = River distance between segments i and j (miles)
  λ    = Distance decay parameter (calibrated: 150 miles)

Connectivity Rules:
  - Adjacent segments: direct connection
  - Lock/dam: flow connection
  - Tributary junction: network connection
Reduced Form:
Y_t = (I - ρ·W)⁻¹ · [c + Φ₁·Y_(t-1) + ... + Φₚ·Y_(t-p) + θ·W·Y_(t-1) + ε_t]

Spatial Multiplier Effect:
  ∂Y_i,t / ∂ε_j,t = [(I - ρ·W)⁻¹]_ij

Total Effect = Direct Effect + Indirect Effect (spillover)
Estimation via Maximum Likelihood:
L(Φ, ρ, θ, Σ) = -Tk/2·ln(2π) + T·ln|I - ρ·W| - T/2·ln|Σ|
                 - 1/2·Σ_t ε_t'·Σ⁻¹·ε_t

Where:
  T = Number of time periods
  k = Number of segments
  |I - ρ·W| = Spatial Jacobian determinant
Spatial Parameter (ρ): 0.42 (strong spatial dependence)
Distance Decay (λ): 150 miles optimal
RMSE Improvement: 22% vs standard VAR
Spillover Effects: 35% of total variation explained by spatial factors
Forecast Accuracy: $0.66/ton RMSE
Economic Value: $2.8M annual cost reduction through improved forecasts

Use Cases

1. Harvest Season Route Optimization

Client: Major grain trading company with 1,500 barge movements annually

Scenario: Fall harvest season (Sept-Nov) with peak demand and potential lock congestion at key chokepoints.

Analysis:

  • Forecasted 12-18 hour delays at Locks 27, 26, and 25 during peak weeks
  • Identified alternative routing through Illinois River with 8-hour delay
  • Calculated cost differential: $1,200/hr main channel vs $950/hr alternative
Result: $4.2M annual savings through dynamic route selection during peak periods

2. Infrastructure Investment Analysis

Client: Port authority evaluating lock expansion project

Scenario: Proposed $180M expansion of Lock 25 to reduce average delays from 14 hours to 6 hours.

Analysis:

  • Current annual delay costs: $28M across all users
  • Projected reduction: $18M annual savings
  • Secondary benefits: $6M from reduced congestion at adjacent segments
  • Payback period: 7.5 years
Result: Investment approved with documented $24M annual benefit and 12% IRR

3. Real-Time Fleet Positioning

Client: Barge line operator with 120 vessel fleet

Scenario: Weekly positioning decisions for empty barges returning from Gulf to upper river terminals.

Analysis:

  • 7-day Spatial VAR forecasts predict rate spikes in Upper Mississippi
  • Model captures spillover from low water on Missouri River
  • Optimal positioning: move 40 barges to segment 18 vs planned segment 22
Result: $320K weekly revenue increase from superior positioning accuracy

4. Seasonal Contract Pricing

Client: Agricultural cooperative negotiating annual freight contracts

Scenario: Setting fixed-price contracts for 800,000 tons of grain shipments across 12 months.

Analysis:

  • Historical seasonal pattern: 18% rate premium Sept-Nov vs base
  • Drought forecast suggests 25% premium in 2026 harvest
  • Model projects $14.50/ton average vs market expectation of $13.80/ton
  • Recommended indexed contract with caps at $16.00/ton
Result: Avoided $560K in unexpected costs through accurate seasonal forecasting

5. Lock Closure Contingency Planning

Client: Regional grain exporters facing scheduled lock maintenance

Scenario: 21-day closure of Lock 27 for emergency repairs with 14-day advance notice.

Analysis:

  • Spatial VAR model predicts rate surge: +$8.50/ton in affected segments
  • Spillover effects: +$3.20/ton in adjacent segments from diverted traffic
  • Identified modal shift threshold: rail competitive above $19/ton
  • Optimal strategy: pre-position 200 loads before closure, switch 150 loads to rail
Result: $1.8M cost avoidance vs reactive strategy, maintained delivery schedules

6. Weather Impact Assessment

Client: Trading desk managing physical grain positions

Scenario: NOAA forecasts below-normal precipitation for Upper Mississippi basin (May-July).

Analysis:

  • Historical correlation: -0.68 between river stage and freight rates
  • Low water forecast suggests 12-foot draft restrictions vs normal 14-foot
  • Rate impact model: +$4.80/ton for restricted draft (15% load reduction)
  • Duration estimate: 8-12 weeks based on rainfall patterns
Result: $2.1M P&L gain from proactive freight hedging and position adjustment
