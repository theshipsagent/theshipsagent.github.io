Executive Summary

Florida represents the second-largest cement consuming state in the US and the fourth-largest producing state, making it a critical market for cement distributors. SESCO Cement Corp's new Port Redwing terminal in Gibsonton positions the company to capture significant market share in the Tampa Bay region, which receives approximately 75% of Florida's cement imports.

Precast segment represents opportunity for both gray and white cement:

  • Specialty products: Terrazzo, custom finishes requiring white cement
  • Technical support differentiator (mix design assistance, quality control)

Methodology

  • Trade Flow Analysis: Aggregated 2,706 import manifests by consignee, shipper, port, and origin country
  • Market Sizing: Florida production capacity from GEM database + import volumes from Panjiva
  • Trucking Radius Calculation: Port Redwing center point at 50-55 mph average highway speed
  • Customer Identification: Industry associations, trade publications, Panjiva consignee data, public company filings
  • Ready-Mix Plant Database: Combined industry databases (NRMCA, state DOT, Google Maps validation)

Data Limitations

  • White cement volumes not separately reported in most government sources (estimated from industry sources)
  • Terminal capacities not publicly disclosed by all operators (estimated from visible infrastructure)
  • Market share by company estimated from import/production data (not audited financials)
  • Customer volumes are proprietary; estimates based on industry standards and comparable companies
  • Trucking zones are approximate; actual delivery times vary by traffic, route conditions
