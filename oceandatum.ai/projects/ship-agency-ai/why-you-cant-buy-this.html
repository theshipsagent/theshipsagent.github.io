FIRST: UNDERSTANDING WHAT WE ARE ACTUALLY TALKING ABOUT

Why This Section Exists

Before evaluating what can and cannot be purchased, it helps to understand what "AI for ship agency" actually means. This is not about chatbots answering FAQs. This is about autonomous systems that do operational work — reading emails, extracting data, updating schedules, making routing decisions, and learning from outcomes. Understanding the difference between what vendors claim and what actually exists requires knowing what "agentic AI" means.

What "Agentic AI" Means (And Does Not Mean)

NOT Agentic: Chatbot

What it does: Answers scripted questions. "What are your office hours?" "Where is my vessel?"

Ship agency value: Nearly zero. Agents do not need a chatbot. They need something that processes 5,000 emails/day.

NOT Agentic: Copilot

What it does: Helps a human work faster. Summarizes emails. Drafts replies. Suggests actions.

Ship agency value: Marginal. Still requires human to read summary, make decision, and manually execute in 5+ systems.

ACTUALLY Agentic: Autonomous Agent

What it does: Reads email. Extracts structured data. Updates schedule state. Evaluates impact. Distributes notifications or escalates to human based on confidence level. Learns from corrections.

Ship agency value: Transformative. Eliminates 80%+ of manual data transposition. Elevates humans from data entry to decision-making.

The Transposition Problem: Why This Matters

The core problem in ship agency is not a missing software tool. It is the gap between tools. An operator spends 5–8 hours per day manually copying the same information between email, agency software, spreadsheets, accounting, WhatsApp, and phone calls. One ETA update gets entered or communicated 4–7 times. Across 18 active port calls, that is 300–500 manual transpositions per day.

THE TEST: EVERY VENDOR GETS THE SAME ONE

Before evaluating 20+ platforms, we defined a single, objective test. This is not a trick question. This is the most basic operation a ship agent performs dozens of times per day. Any platform claiming "AI for maritime" should handle this trivially.

The Ship Agency AI Test

Can your system autonomously process this email and execute ALL required actions without human intervention?

Subject: Revised ETA - MV OCEAN STAR

Good morning,

Please be advised MV OCEAN STAR revised ETA Berth 7 now 1400 UTC
15 Feb due weather delay. Previous ETA was 0800 same date.
Please advise terminal and update all parties.

Regards,
Owners Operations

Required Actions (All Must Be Automatic):

  1. Classify: Identify this is an ETA update (not crew change, not invoice, not routine confirmation)
  2. Extract: Vessel name (MV OCEAN STAR), new ETA (1400 UTC 15 Feb), berth (7), reason (weather delay)
  3. Calculate: Delay magnitude: 6 hours from previous ETA of 0800
  4. Update: Vessel port call schedule state in database
  5. Evaluate: Impact on berth rotation (are other vessels affected by 6-hour shift?)
  6. Classify urgency: Based on delay magnitude, time of receipt, and berth conflict risk
  7. If high confidence (routine, no conflicts): Autonomously distribute update to terminal, principal, pilot, and other stakeholders
  8. If medium confidence (potential conflict): Escalate to human with pre-assembled decision brief
  9. Log: Record all actions taken. Learn pattern for future similar scenarios.
0 / 20+
Platforms pass this test. Not a single commercial product can execute all 9 steps autonomously.
