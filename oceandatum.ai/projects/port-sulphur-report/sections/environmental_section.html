Executive Summary

The former Freeport-McMoRan Port Sulphur facility presents significant environmental liabilities stemming from 72 years of Frasch sulfur processing operations (1933-2005). The site carries exceptionally high environmental liability risks due to the combination of legacy contamination, extreme flood vulnerability, rapid coastal land loss, and EPA Non-NPL Superfund oversight.

Critical Environmental Findings

High Risk

Operational History

72 years of Frasch sulfur processing operations (1933-2005) created extensive legacy contamination across processing areas, storage zones, and transportation routes.

  • Sulfur vating/storage areas with highest contamination concentrations
  • Processing areas with equipment foundations and spill zones
  • Transportation routes at loading/unloading areas
  • Historical waste disposal areas
Critical Risk

2009 Acidic Water Release

Major environmental incident released 5.8 million pounds of acidic water with pH as low as 1.7, affecting 1.5 miles of waterways downstream into Gainard Woods Canal.

  • Date: April 27 - June 15, 2009
  • Cause: Leak in Vat Line pipeline near Highway 23
  • pH Readings: 1.7-1.73 SU (highly acidic)
  • Status: Remediated, closed July 10, 2009 (LDEQ Doc 6484954)
High Risk

Soil Contamination

Elemental sulfur concentrations up to 50,900 mg/kg observed, with typical pH 2.5-3.5 (highly acidic/phytotoxic). Heavy metals mobilized by acid leaching.

  • Elemental Sulfur: Max 50,900 mg/kg (threshold: 10,000 mg/kg)
  • Soil pH: 2.5-3.5 (threshold: 3.0)
  • Heavy Metals: Iron, aluminum, zinc, lead, cadmium, arsenic
  • Hot Spots: Require removal where exceeding 10,000 mg/kg
Critical Risk

Hurricane Flood Vulnerability

22 feet of flooding during Hurricane Katrina (2005) at site with base elevation of only 8 feet above sea level. Nearly all structures destroyed, population displaced from 3,000 to minimal.

  • Katrina (2005): 22 ft storm surge - catastrophic
  • Ida (2021): 9-14 ft storm surge in Plaquemines Parish
  • Base Elevation: 8 feet above sea level
  • Insurance: High-cost flood insurance mandatory
Medium Risk

Regulatory Status

No active permits as of 2025, but EPA Non-NPL Superfund oversight continues. Previous RCRA Small Quantity Generator status now inactive.

  • RCRA Status: Inactive (was Active SQG 220-2,200 lbs/month)
  • Air Permits: Permanently closed minor source
  • Water Permits: All terminated (LA0004898, LAR05P368, LAR10G702)
  • EPA Oversight: Active Non-NPL Superfund (EPA Region 6)
Critical Risk

Coastal Hazards & Sea Level Rise

Plaquemines Parish experiencing world's highest relative sea level rise rates with projected 1-3 feet subsidence by 2100. Land loss equivalent to football field every 38 minutes statewide.

  • Land Loss Rate: Football field every 38 minutes (Louisiana)
  • Subsidence: 1-3 feet projected by 2100
  • Sea Level Rise: World's highest relative rates
  • Site Risk: May become isolated island surrounded by open water
High Risk

Groundwater Contamination

Acid mine drainage characteristics with pH below 3.5, elevated metals, and potential migration to Mississippi River due to site's proximity and shallow groundwater.

  • pH: Below 3.5 (acid mine drainage)
  • Metals: Elevated iron, aluminum, trace metals
  • Sulfate: Extremely high concentrations
  • Migration Risk: Potential discharge to Mississippi River
Medium Risk

2016 Diesel Release

Diesel release discovered July 2016 from former Above Storage Tank (AST) location. Ongoing exceedances in soil and groundwater monitoring wells in limited areas.

  • Discovery: July 18, 2016
  • Contaminants: Diesel hydrocarbons, PAHs, lead, arsenic
  • Status: No Further Action for primary area (LDEQ RECAP)
  • Remaining: Exceedances in Area B-3, wells TW-3, TW-13, TW-14

Remediation & Settlement History

2009 Emergency Response

April 27 - July 10, 2009 Completed

Emergency response to 5.8 million pound acidic water release. Comprehensive remediation including neutralization, berm construction, pipeline replacement, and 24-hour monitoring.

Closeout Date: July 10, 2009
Closeout Document: LDEQ-EDMS Document 6484954
Infrastructure Installed:
  • Berms in golf course ditch
  • pH monitoring equipment downstream
  • Fencing along east and west sides of golf course
  • Warning signs along canal
  • Replacement Vat Line pipeline under Highway 23
Remediation Actions:
  • Neutralized 18 drainage ditches
  • Excavated and replaced contaminated soil near highway
  • Elevated east side of ring levee by ~18 inches
  • Raised levee in southwest weir area to consistent height
  • Replaced weir with continuation of earthen ring levee

2014 Settlement Agreement

Environmental Liabilities for Prospective Purchaser

Contamination Risks

  • Legacy sulfur processing contamination in waterfront soil and sediments
  • Old barge slip and filled sulfur mine canal with subsurface contamination risks
  • High water table with potential contamination migration from historical operations
  • Potential CERCLA reopener liability if contamination worse than understood

Maintenance Obligations

  • EPA oversight coordination for Non-NPL Superfund site
  • Potential inheritance of groundwater monitoring wells and treatment systems
  • Cap maintenance for any existing contamination caps
  • Institutional controls with potential land use restrictions
  • Ongoing monitoring from 2009 incident infrastructure (17+ years old as of 2026)
  • Storm water infrastructure maintenance

Regulatory Obligations

  • All Appropriate Inquiries (Phase I ESA) required before purchase for BFPP protection
  • Potential Phase II ESA required for contamination characterization
  • Coordination with EPA Region 6 and LDEQ for historical permit data
  • Compliance with institutional controls if present
  • Five-year review clause if contamination left in place

Financial Risks

  • Substantial reserves required for ongoing monitoring and maintenance (15-20% of development costs)
  • Specialized environmental insurance coverage required
  • High-cost flood insurance mandatory (FEMA high-risk zone)
  • Potential cleanup costs if CERCLA reopener triggered
  • Development restrictions may lower property value
  • Bona Fide Prospective Purchaser (BFPP) designation recommended for liability protection

Recommended Actions for Due Diligence

Immediate Actions (Pre-Purchase)

  1. Conduct comprehensive Phase I Environmental Site Assessment before any transaction
  2. Perform Phase II ESA with soil and groundwater sampling if Phase I identifies RECs
  3. Contact EPA Region 6 for formal public records request on historical permit data
  4. Contact LDEQ for complete file on Agency Interest No. 17227
  5. Verify BFPP eligibility requirements and pursue designation for liability protection

Risk Assessment

  1. Obtain specialized environmental insurance coverage quotes
  2. Conduct flood risk assessment and obtain mandatory flood insurance quotes
  3. Review any recorded Environmental Covenants or Notice of Residual Contamination on title
  4. Verify existence and condition of any institutional controls or engineering controls (caps, barriers)
  5. Inspect 2009 remediation infrastructure for condition after 17+ years

Long-Term Planning

  1. Assess potential need for ongoing groundwater monitoring programs
  2. Budget for environmental contingency (15-20% of development costs)
  3. Engage environmental counsel for liability assessment and transaction structure
  4. Develop long-term coastal adaptation strategy for sea level rise and subsidence
  5. Establish relationship with EPA Region 6 and LDEQ for ongoing coordination

Critical Buyer Warning

This site carries exceptionally high environmental liability risks due to the combination of legacy contamination, extreme flood vulnerability, rapid coastal land loss, and EPA Superfund oversight. Any purchase requires comprehensive environmental due diligence, substantial financial reserves for ongoing monitoring and maintenance obligations, and specialized environmental insurance coverage.
