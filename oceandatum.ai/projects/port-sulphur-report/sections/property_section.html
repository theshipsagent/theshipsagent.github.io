Ownership & Operational Timeline (1932-2025)

The Port Sulphur terminal has a 93-year history spanning sulfur mining operations, corporate transitions, natural disaster, environmental remediation, and current availability for industrial redevelopment.

1932: Sulfur Rights Acquisition

Freeport Sulphur Company acquired sulfur rights for Lake Grande Ecaille, establishing the foundation for regional sulfur operations.

1933: Facility Construction & Operations Commence

Port Sulphur facility construction began; operations commenced as logistics, refining, storage and shipping hub supporting the Frasch Process sulfur extraction.

1933-1978: Primary Sulfur Mining Period

45 years of primary sulfur mine operations at Grande Ecaille (10 miles west). Port Sulphur served as the processing and export hub, handling molten sulfur in insulated tanks and "vatting" operations.

1981: Corporate Merger

McMoRan Oil & Gas merged with Freeport Minerals to create Freeport-McMoRan Inc. (FTX), consolidating operations.

1980s-1990s: Economic Decline

Operations declined due to recovered sulfur from oil and gas refining creating market competition. Processing continued but at reduced capacity.

Early 2000s: Cessation of Operations

Operations ceased entirely due to market competition, leaving legacy infrastructure in place.

2005: Hurricane Katrina Destruction

Hurricane Katrina destroyed remaining sulfur facility infrastructure with 22-foot storm surge and 75 knot winds. The disaster marked the end of the industrial era at the site.

Post-2005: Environmental Remediation

Environmental assessments and remediation completed under Louisiana DEQ's Risk Evaluation/Corrective Action Program (RECAP). Site cleared for future development.

2025: Current Status - Available for Development

Property listed as remediated industrial waterfront asset with significant expansion potential. Strategic location for LNG, container terminal, or bulk cargo operations.

  • Storm Surge: 22 feet

  • Damage: Destroyed remaining infrastructure
  • Impact: Created 82 sq mi new water areas in parish

Hurricane Ida (2021)

  • Storm Surge: 9-14 feet
  • Impact: Significant flooding throughout region

Hurricane Betsy (1965) & Camille (1969)

  • Site Impact: No flooding at Port Sulphur site

  • Flood Insurance: Estimated $100,000+ annually for high-risk zone coverage
