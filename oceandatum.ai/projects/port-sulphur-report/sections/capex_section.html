Investment Overview

The Port Sulphur Terminal development requires a total project investment ranging from $27.95M to $52.65M, with a base case estimate of $35.0M. The project timeline spans 39-81 months (3.25-6.75 years) across four major phases: due diligence, environmental remediation, permitting, and construction.

The property acquisition of the 107-acre site at Mile 39 on the Mississippi River is valued at $14.7M ($137,500/acre), securing 10,693 linear feet of river frontage with existing 52-foot draft capacity and pre-existing dock infrastructure.

Financing Structures & Opportunities

Multiple financing pathways exist for port and terminal infrastructure development, combining public and private capital sources. The Port Sulphur Terminal project is eligible for several tax-advantaged and federal credit programs due to its maritime infrastructure classification.

Municipal Revenue Bonds

Tax-exempt bonds for docks/wharves under IRC ยง142 (exempt-facility bonds). Repayment from facility rents, fees, or system net revenues.

  • Eligible Use: Docks, wharves, mass commuting facilities
  • Tax Treatment: Tax-exempt interest for investors
  • Repayment Source: Project revenues or port authority system revenues
  • Example: Port Authority consolidated bonds backed by system revenues

Private Activity Bonds (PABs)

Tax-exempt bonds issued by public authority for private projects meeting IRC ยง142 requirements.

  • Eligible Facilities: Docks, wharves, transportation infrastructure
  • Volume Cap: State allocation required
  • Tax Benefit: Tax-exempt financing for private development
  • Structure: Public issuance with private project repayment obligation

Lease-Revenue Bonds

Bonds repaid from pledged lease revenues under long-term operating agreements.

  • Security: Dedicated lease payment stream
  • Example: Fernandina OHPA bond refinancing (2018)
  • Advantage: Matches debt service to operational cash flows

Public-Private Partnership (P3)

Availability-payment or revenue-risk concessions with long-term private operation.

  • Models: DBFOM (Design-Build-Finance-Operate-Maintain)
  • Example: Port of Miami Tunnel (30-year availability payments)
  • Risk Allocation: Construction risk to private partner, revenue risk varies

TIFIA Loans

Federal credit assistance for transportation infrastructure (Transportation Infrastructure Finance and Innovation Act).

  • Terms: Long-term, low-interest federal loans
  • Example: Gerald Desmond Bridge (Long Beach)
  • Advantage: Below-market rates, flexible repayment

RAISE Grants

Federal grant program for transportation projects (formerly TIGER/BUILD grants).

  • Funding Level: Up to 85% federal funding possible
  • Example: Plaquemines rail spur extension ($72M federal of $85M total)
  • Competitive: Nationwide competition, strong regional benefits required
Regional Financing Context: Plaquemines Port Harbor & Terminal District (Louisiana Gateway Port) maintains strong financial position: $116.8M net position, $6M operating revenue (+13% growth), $75.4M capital assets, and $300M bond financing capacity (as of December 31, 2022).
