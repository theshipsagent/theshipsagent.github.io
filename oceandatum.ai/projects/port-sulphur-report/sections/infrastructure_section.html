APM Terminals Container Terminal
(Louisiana Gateway Port)

Planned

Location: Port Sulphur area, West Bank (near existing site)

Phase 1 Area: 200 acres (expandable to 900 acres)

Investment: $500 million (privately funded by APM Terminals/Maersk)

Vessel Capacity: 14,000-18,000 TEU (largest Panama Canal ships)

On-Dock Rail: Yes, unit trains up to 100 cars, 20-30 trains/week initial

Timeline: Permits approved June 2025, groundbreaking Q2 2026, operational 2028

Strategic Advantages:

  • No air draft restrictions from bridges
  • Proximity to LNG facilities
  • 3 hours shorter round trip than nearest proposed terminal
  • Widest ship turning radius
  • 30-40% drayage cost savings via on-dock rail

Projected to become 5th largest U.S. port by tonnage within five years of operation.

Rail Infrastructure & Connectivity

Current Status: No direct rail access. Nearest connection is New Orleans & Gulf Coast Railway (NOGC) terminus at Myrtle Grove, 8 miles north of Port Sulphur.

New Orleans & Gulf Coast Railway (NOGC)

Classification: Class III Shortline

Length: 32 miles

Route: Westwego (UP interchange) → Belle Chasse → Myrtle Grove

Operations: 20+ customers, up to 3 trains/day

Speed: 10-15 MPH

Capacity Utilization: Less than 10%

Commodities: Petroleum products, oils, chemicals, food products, grains, steel

Class I Access: Direct UP interchange; BNSF, CSXT, CN, CPKC, NS via NOPB

Port Sulphur Rail Spur Extension - $85M Project

Project: 32-mile rail spur from Port Sulphur to Belle Chasse, connecting to NOGC Railway and national Class I network.

Investment: $85 million total

Federal Funding: 85% ($72M RAISE grants allocated 2025)

State Match: PPMTD unrestricted fund

Sponsor: Plaquemines Port Harbor & Terminal District

Design Study: Completed May 2025

Preliminary Engineering: $10M approved June 2025

Construction Bids: Expected Q4 2025

Completion: 2028-2029

Benefits:

  • Highway 23 Relief: Reduce truck traffic by 500,000 loads per year
  • National Connectivity: Direct access to Midwest/heartland markets
  • Commodity Support: Grains, metals, consumer goods, containers
  • Environmental Review: FERC reviews for wetland terrain

Approvals: Parish ordinance (August 2024), preliminary engineering funding (June 2025), public meetings on community impacts, noise, and wetland mitigation (June 26, 2025).
