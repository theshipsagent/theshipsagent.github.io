Integrated Permitting Strategy

Port Sulphur Terminal development requires coordination across multiple regulatory authorities with overlapping but distinct jurisdictions. The integrated permitting approach treats Section 408 permission as the primary regulatory pathway, with Section 10 and Section 404 permits processed concurrently under USACE New Orleans District coordination.

2025 Programmatic Environmental Assessment

Provides standardized evaluation criteria that streamline the review process. Appendix B contains specific engineering criteria for revetment interactions, reducing the need for project-specific studies when standard approaches are applicable.

Permit Types by Jurisdiction

Distribution of required permits across federal, state, and local regulatory levels.

Federal Permits (Primary)

  • Section 408 Permission (USACE New Orleans District)
    Modification of federal flood control/navigation projects
  • Section 10 Permit (USACE New Orleans District)
    Work in or affecting navigable waters
  • Section 404 Permit (USACE New Orleans District)
    Discharge of dredged/fill material into wetlands (if applicable)

  • Environmental Compliance (Louisiana DEQ - LAC 33)
    Soil remediation, groundwater protection, RBCA protocols
  • Building Permits (Plaquemines Parish)
    Construction of terminal facilities and structures
  • Development & Zoning Approvals (Plaquemines Parish)

Required Environmental Studies

Sequential environmental assessment process required before comprehensive permit package submission. Phase progression depends on findings at each stage.

1

Phase I Environmental Site Assessment

Required

Purpose: Historical review and visual assessment

Scope: Review of historical site usage, potential contamination sources, regulatory database search, site reconnaissance

Status: Not yet initiated - immediate action recommended

2

Phase II Environmental Site Assessment

Conditional

Purpose: Soil/groundwater sampling and analysis

Scope: Subsurface investigation, chemical analysis, contamination delineation

Trigger: Positive findings in Phase I ESA

3

Risk Assessment

Conditional

Purpose: Human health and ecological risk evaluation

Regulatory Driver: EPA Regional Screening Levels (RSLs), LDEQ RBCA protocols

Trigger: Contamination identified in Phase II

4-6

Remedial Investigation, Feasibility Study & Design

Conditional

Purpose: Detailed contamination characterization, technology evaluation, and engineering design for selected remedy

Regulatory Driver: EPA guidance (EPA-542-R-07-013), LDEQ approval required

Trigger: Risk assessment determines remediation necessary

Timeline Optimization Strategies

Six critical success factors for reducing permitting timeline and costs through proactive coordination and comprehensive preparation.

Critical Success Factors

  • Early Pre-Application Consultation
    Understand regulatory requirements before design commitment; identify potential issues early when modifications are most cost-effective
  • Comprehensive Application Packages
    Reduce review cycles by providing complete documentation upfront; minimize information requests that delay processing
  • Proper Technical Documentation
    Avoid design revisions and reduce back-and-forth with regulators through thorough initial submittals

Streamlining Benefits

  • Concurrent Permit Processing
    Single point coordination through USACE New Orleans District; reduced waiting between sequential permits
  • Programmatic EA Standards
    Use standardized criteria from Appendix B; reduce project-specific studies when standard approaches applicable
  • Early Environmental Assessment
    Prevent late-stage discoveries; avoid costly remediation delays that extend timeline significantly

Immediate Actions & Next Steps

Immediate Actions (0-3 months)

  1. Initiate Pre-Application Consultation
    Contact USACE New Orleans District to schedule preliminary meeting and discuss project scope, regulatory requirements, and timeline expectations
  2. Commission Phase I Environmental Site Assessment
    Engage qualified environmental consultant to conduct historical review, database search, and site reconnaissance
  3. Review 2025 Programmatic EA
    Study Appendix B engineering criteria for revetment interactions and standardized evaluation requirements
  4. Engage Louisiana DEQ
    Initial coordination regarding state permitting requirements under LAC 33

Near-Term Actions (3-6 months)

  1. Complete Phase II ESA (if triggered)
    Conduct subsurface investigation and chemical analysis if Phase I identifies concerns
  2. Develop Preliminary Engineering Designs
    Incorporate Programmatic EA criteria and USACE feedback from pre-application consultation
  3. Initiate Parish Coordination
    Begin discussions with Plaquemines Parish regarding local permits and timing
  4. Prepare Integrated Permit Application Strategy
    Develop comprehensive plan for concurrent Section 408, Section 10, and Section 404 submissions

Documentation Priorities for Permit Applications

  • Comprehensive Technical Documentation
    Engineering analysis, design calculations, construction methodology, structural plans
  • Engineering Drawings Meeting Appendix B Standards
    Revetment interaction details, navigation safety features, dock structural details
  • Environmental Assessment Reports
    Phase I/II ESA results, risk assessment findings, remedial action plans (if needed)
  • Revetment Interaction Design Documentation
    Bank stability analysis, scour protection, revetment penetration procedures
  • Navigation Safety Analysis
    Vessel maneuverability studies, current velocity analysis, berthing simulation results
  • Wetlands Delineation (if applicable)
    Jurisdictional determination, impact assessment, mitigation proposals
  • Species Consultation Documentation
    Protected species surveys, critical habitat assessments, impact mitigation measures
